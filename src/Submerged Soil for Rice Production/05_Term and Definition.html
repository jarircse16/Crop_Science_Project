Term & Definition
Aerenchyma :
A porous structure in the stem and roots of plants adapted to
water-logged environments. Gases, like oxygen and carbon dioxide from the
atmosphere, pass through the aerenchyma to the plant roots and vice versa.
Aerobic :
In the presence of oxygen; Any organism needing oxygen to survive is
called aerobic. An organism growing without oxygen is anaerobic.
Anaerobic :
lacking the presence of free oxygen.

Dibble :
A traditional method of planting where a pointed stick is used to make
a hole in the ground. Seeds are placed in the hole and then covered.

Eutrophication :
When excess nutrients (particularly phosphorus and/or
nitrogen) cause abundant biological growth in an aquatic ecosystem. Excess
growth can result in oxygen depletion that is harmful or fatal to other organisms.
Facultative anaerobe :
An organism that can survive with or without oxygen.
Leaching :
When soluble materials are moved (usually downward) in soil
because of water movement.
Lignin :
A complex carbon compound that is part of the rigid cell wall in plants.
Lowland :
A method of rice production where soil is submerged for part or all of
the growing season. Lowland rice production can be irrigated or rainfed and
typically involves puddling of soil. Refer to Module 1 Lessons 1, 2, & 3 for more
information.
Nematode :
Roundworms of the Nematoda phylum which live freely or as
parasites in soil, water, and inside other organisms.

Respiration :
A process where glucose and other molecules are converted into
usable energy.
Rhizosphere :
Soil that immediately surrounds plant roots. Rhizosphere
contains many microorganisms that are supported by the presence of the roots.
Soil that is not part of the rhizosphere can be called bulk soil.
Soil aggregates :
Groups of soil particles bound together by moist clay, organic
matter, and thin fungal strands.
Soil moisture content :

e amount of water in soil. It can be expressed by

Soil pH :
An indicator of the acidity or alkalinity of soil and expressed as a
number between 1 to 14. pH < 7 is acidic; pH > 7 is basic or alkaline; pH near 7
is neutral; Soil pH is an important characteristic inﬂuencing many other
chemical and physical variables of soil.
Soil substrates :
An organic or inorganic substance that is acted upon during a
reaction and becomes something else in the process.
Submerged :
covered by water; ﬂooded.
Upland :
A method of rice production where soil remains aerobic (it is not
intentionally submerged). Rainfed upland rice production typically begins with
the onset of rains. Refer to Module 1 Lessons 1 & 4 for more information.
